Taxism (noun) \ ˈtak-ˌsi-zəm \

An affinity for traditional taxi services over ride-sharing services such as Uber or Lyft, rooted in a desire to preserve the unique culture and history of taxis. Taxists may advocate for higher pay for taxi drivers and resist the perceived homogenization of the transportation industry by large, capitalist organizations.
